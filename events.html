Workshop on Flutter

A two-day Flutter workshop was conducted at Mar Ivanios College by the department of Computer Science in association with ICFOSS on February 29 and March 1, 2024, tailored for S4 Computer Science students to enhance their mobile application development skills. The workshop, led by Mr. K S Abhishek, a dedicated Flutter Developer, commenced with an introductory speech by Flickson J, expressing excitement to welcome Mr. Abhishek and highlighting his expertise in crafting exceptional user experiences. Mr. Abhishek's sessions covered a range of topics, including setting up a Flutter project, Dart programming basics, and advanced options in VSCode for Flutter development. His hands-on approach, where he personally assisted each participant and addressed their queries, was highly appreciated. The workshop, held at the Computer Science Department Lab, provided a conducive environment for collaborative learning, enabling participants to engage in practical exercises and exchange ideas. The event's success was attributed to Mr. Abhishek's exceptional training, the faculty's support, and the enthusiastic participation of the students. The vote of thanks was delivered by Hanan Faiza N, who expressed gratitude to Mr. Abhishek, the faculty, and the participants for making the workshop a memorable and enriching experience.

National Symposium on Language Computing

The symposium on language computing, organized by the Departments of Computer Science and Malayalam at Mar Ivanios College, in collaboration with ICFOSS, was held from January 23 to 25, 2024. The event began with an inaugural ceremony featuring speeches by prominent academicians and experts, including Dr. Sunil TT from ICFOSS. Sessions covered a range of topics, such as recent advancements in language technology by Dr. Nisha Varghese, text classification by Dr. Sweety Bakyarani, and named entity recognition by Ms. M Ramla. Workshops on LaTeX and research paper preparation were conducted by Ms. Meharuniza Nazeem and Ms. Arya Sreekumar. The final day included a session on the future of language technologies by Dr. Rajeev RR and concluded with a closing ceremony highlighting the symposium's educational significance and its role in advancing language computing for Indian languages, particularly Malayalam.
